about Dibulla

The municipality of Dibulla is located in a geographical position that potentiates the various socioeconomic lines of the region: on the one hand, it has the influence of the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta, which allows it to have a considerable variety of thermal peaks; on the other is the Caribbean Sea that allows you to have access to marine wealth. On the other hand, the municipality of Dibulla benefits from the passage of the Troncal del Caribe along its length, interconnecting several of its districts with the rest of La Guajira and towards the Caribbean Coast through the Magdalena.

The average temperature is 30°C with relative humidity between 70 and 75%. Rainfall is 1,200 mm per year, with high evaporation reaching 1,495 mm, which generates regional climatological imbalance. The climate is predominantly dry. The region is defined as an area with two well-defined periods: rainy, between the months of April to June and September to November, dry between the months of July to August and December to March.
